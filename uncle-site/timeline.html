Life Timeline

A life told through seasons and milestones

A simple timeline of family roots, work, church life, and enduring interests.

1940s

Early beginnings

Born into a family that valued steadiness, faith, good work, and modesty. Early years were shaped by home, church, and the routines of a close-knit community.

1950s

School years and early character

Quiet in manner, observant by nature, and thoughtful from an early age. These were years of learning, helping, and noticing the details others often missed.

1960s

Formation and calling

Adulthood opened into wider responsibilities: work, service, faith community, and the beginnings of lifelong interests that would deepen over the years.

1970s

Marriage and family life

A household was established, children arrived, and ordinary life took on its rich, demanding shape. Much of what mattered most was built quietly in these years.

1980s

Work, routine, and reliability

These years were marked by dependable effort, practical wisdom, and a growing reputation for listening carefully before speaking.

1990s

A deepening interest in adult ADHD

Over time, attention turned more intentionally toward adult ADHD: not as a matter of public identity, but as a field of practical insight, patient observation, and tools that might genuinely help people understand themselves better.

2000s

Church reflections and recorded talk

A major landmark from this period is a 2008 church talk, recorded on video, where much of his character comes through: measured, thoughtful, calm, and grounded.

2010s

Grandchildren, memory, and legacy

Family life widened into new generations. The pace softened, but the meaning of daily life grew clearer: stories, visits, church, and small acts of care.

Today

A life worth preserving well

This website gathers photographs, recorded reflections, family notes, and resource materials in one place, so that what is quiet and meaningful is not easily lost.
